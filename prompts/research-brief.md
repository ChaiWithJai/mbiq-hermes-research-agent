# Research brief contract

Create one editorial research brief. It is an evidence packet, not finished brand copy.

## Required sections

1. **Assignment**: event ID, working question, intended Queens community, and why this matters now.
2. **Story spine**: one plain sentence that states the strongest supportable story.
3. **What we know**: five to ten material facts. Put a source link beside every fact.
4. **Queens connection**: the specific people, places, institutions, or histories that make this a Queens story.
5. **Community voices**: first-person or community-run sources, what each contributes, and who is still missing.
6. **Tensions and open questions**: disputed facts, missing records, risk of flattening a community, and follow-up reporting needs.
7. **Story routes**: three possible treatments. Include a working headline, opening scene, audience, evidence needed, and why MBIQ has standing to tell it.
8. **Source ledger**: title, publisher or holder, URL, source type, date, access date, claim supported, and any limitation.
9. **Editorial check**: answer yes or no for Queens specificity, community as protagonist, primary-source support, claim-level citations, calibrated certainty, and human review required.

## Minimum source bar

- Five fetched and read sources.
- Two primary sources.
- One Queens-specific primary source.
- One community-run or first-person source.
- One source that can challenge or complicate the initial angle.

If the bar cannot be met, return a gap report instead of pretending the brief is ready.
